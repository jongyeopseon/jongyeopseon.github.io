How to trust a photo in the age of AI
Lutra, 2026. HOA Camera. Patent KR 10-2614829.

Keywords: Blockchain × Trust

There are two ways to fight fake images: detection and provenance. Detection is a losing race, and provenance is stricter and extends beyond photos to any data, text, audio, or video. HOA takes the provenance path: the camera cryptographically signs sensor data the instant a photo is taken and anchors its origin on a blockchain, so a real photograph can prove where it came from.

Rather than releasing the technology alone, we built it into a designed camera to make it tangible. Unveiled at TechCrunch Disrupt 2025, where it drew interest from companies including Sony. Now in production, launching October 2026, and you can check it on this site (under construction) (hoa.camera).

Artists’ AI agent that preserves their identity
Lutra, 2025. Tech licensing to Urban Complex.

Keywords: Diffusion AI × Identity

Artists who use AI face the question from both sides: they wonder “is this still my work?”, and others deny the authorship outright. We looked into how different countries recognize copyright for AI-assisted work, and reframed the model so the artist controls it actively rather than passively waiting on the AI's output, redefining AI as an assistant rather than a creator. We designed the UX so that artists would naturally perceive the AI as a tool.

The technology was successfully licensed, and Urban Complex uses it in production today. Conducted as a joint TIPS R&D project between a financial-engineering lab at Yonsei University and Urban Complex; as Urban Complex's technology partner, I served as PI and led the research.

Character personalization, at the edge of the uncanny
POSTECH, 2024. Capstone Design Project, 1st Place.

Keywords: AI Image Personalization × Identity

A character-personalization photo booth that started from a playful idea: "What if I became Mickey Mouse? Wouldn't that make for a more fun keepsake?" Because the character sits at the center of the project, its own identity has to survive, so the real question was how far the resemblance should go: which features, from hairstyle down, make people recognize themselves without the result feeling wrong.

With an HCI PhD researcher at KAIST, we ran about four months of consumer studies and surveys to find that point. Won 1st place in the POSTECH EE capstone design course, then commercialized as Character Studio.

A SaaS web builder with the accessibility even AI tools miss
Lutra, 2024. PageChips.

Keywords: SaaS Web × Accessibility

Today's web builders obsess over how a page looks, so they leave people who are blind or rely on assistive tools without the information they need. Yet accessibility is also one of the easiest ways to guarantee clear, legible design, which helps younger users as much as older ones. Many of our B2B clients run festivals and pop-up stores, spinning up short-lived pages they use once and discard, and that is what led us to build a SaaS page builder.

PageChips (chips.page) puts accessibility first, drawing on what I learned volunteering at the Seoul National School for the Blind, so every block is accessible by construction. At the Goyang International Flower Fair 2024 it reached over 40,000 visitors, and its redesigned site won a warm response from people of all ages.

The safest, most joyful use of NFTs
Lutra, 2022. Clam. CES Innovation Award 2023.

Keywords: NFT × Play

To engineers, NFTs were often used far beyond their technical definition, and dangerously so. Not all of the data actually lived on-chain, so if the seller went bankrupt after your purchase, the image behind your NFT could easily disappear. We believed the answer was to build the best possible use of the technology, and that is Clam.

When NFTs were new, opaque, and hyped, with interest running high even among older generations, we chose to lead the technology's popularization in a good direction: a joyful, safe form. The concept was simple: turning people's experiences into digital keepsakes they truly own.

Honored with a CES Innovation Award 2023 (Software & Mobile Apps) as the youngest Korean honoree. After CES, we signed MOUs with the blockchain subsidiaries of KAKAO and NAVER, and the product grew into a photo-booth form, supplied to institutions and brands. Still running at clamspace.com.

Can machine learning predict EV range? Or can UX?
Hyundai Motor Company, 2021. Big Data Team Internship.

Keywords: Machine Learning × Trust

Range anxiety was a serious complaint: the dash says you can reach Busan, and the car stops near Daegu. I joined the big-data team to fix the Hyundai EV's range prediction with ML, and learned that perfect prediction is impossible, closer to forecasting stocks than measuring fuel. Studying how other makers’ interfaces handle this, I found they present short-horizon information built from the last minutes of driving, detailed enough that drivers see “it’s how I drive” rather than “the number is broken.”

My analysis identified which factors influence range the most, and the conclusion was that the problem must be solved jointly with UX: give drivers verifiable, short-horizon information instead of a single unexplained number.

MBTI × Face: a machine-learning test
POSTECH, 2021. Undergraduate Research Program, Excellence Award.

Keywords: Machine Learning × Identity

Some people claim physiognomy is just statistics. Is that actually true? I tested the claim: MBTI-prediction models trained on celebrities' photos and their publicly known types, reaching 76% accuracy on some traits.

Then I dug into why. The signal was confidence in the photo, its mood, the backgrounds, how heavy the makeup: where you spend your time and how you present yourself correlates with personality and leaks into photos. Not the face itself, a data-driven caution against physiognomic AI.

My role: leading the team, framing the research topic and direction, automating the face-detection and data pipeline, and exploring ML methods to set the approach.

An NLP opinion-analysis engine for bridging generations
POSTECH, 2020. 3rd CCRP Competition Winner.

Keywords: NLP × Accessibility

Recommendation algorithms split public opinion and tip it easily to one side. As a result, conflict and division began spreading across Korea. Generational conflict in particular struck me as very likely rooted in a simple lack of information about one another, and I believed the way to ease it was to give people an objective chance to see where each generation stands. The NLP engine I designed offers exactly that: it classifies sentiment and stance issue by issue and generation by generation, sorting out the claims, key evidence, and comments around the topics society debates most.

The analysis engine itself was built and working, proposed as civic technology and selected in POSTECH's CCRP (Convergence Civilization Renaissance Production) program.

Designing an educational coding humanoid robot for kids
ROBOTIS, 2020. Prototyping Team Internship.

Keywords: Robotics × Play

A commercial line of educational humanoid robots, used in competitions like the STEAM Cup where kids code AI into their machines. My work covered the whole prototype: 3D printing, mechanical design, internal algorithms, and the core calibration question, how much power and range of motion the Dynamixel actuators should allow for a robot that looks cool to kids, invites customization, and battles safely.

I worked on the CEO's direct team, alongside engineers building autonomous robots, and learned a great deal about everything from the overall prototyping stage through to productization, gaining a wide perspective along the way.

A remote robot for the COVID era
POSTECH × DGIST, 2020. 8th K-Hackathon Award.

Keywords: Robotics × Accessibility

When COVID made visiting a place in person impossible, we presented an engineering prototype of a telepresence robot, one that lets you feel present in a space actively and vividly rather than through mere video. We built a robot to visit for you: touring a home before renting it, walking a museum from your couch. TurtleBot-based, with LiDAR and 160° cameras viewable in VR, controlled from an app. Pick a destination and it navigates there on its own, avoiding obstacles.

Awarded at the 8th K-Hackathon immersive-content challenge, and selected for the final live demonstration of the Soljik Challenge.

My role: I was the Team Leader, from setting the direction to the robot design and all of the ROS development.

A vote-info web app for first-time voters
Independent, 2020. Featured on KBS & MBC radio.

Keywords: Web × Accessibility

As a first-time voter I kept asking why my generation ignores elections. My hypothesis: information that once reached everyone on paper is now scattered across the internet, complex and hard to read. With high-school friends, I built a single website that shows each candidate’s essentials as summary cards.

It reached 40,000 visitors, with thousands arriving daily at its peak, and was covered by newspapers and 20+ outlets, including radio interviews on KBS and MBC (govote.netlify.app).

Can blockchain solve low donation participation?
POSTECH, 2019. 2nd CCRP Competition Winner.

Keywords: Blockchain × Trust

I believed low giving comes from low trust in institutions. Operating costs are a fair use of donations; what’s missing is transparency about where the money goes. So I designed a public blockchain ledger that makes a charity’s inflows and outflows verifiable by anyone.

Built a simple working prototype, and the proposal was selected and awarded in POSTECH’s CCRP program.
