Projects

How to trust a photo in the age of AI
Lutra, 2026. HOA Camera. Patent KR 10-2614829. Trust

There are two ways to fight fake images: detection and provenance. Detection is a losing race, and provenance is stricter and extends beyond photos to any data, text, audio, or video. HOA takes the provenance path: the camera cryptographically signs sensor data the instant a photo is taken and anchors its origin on a blockchain, so a real photograph can prove where it came from.

Rather than releasing the technology alone, we built it into a designed camera to make it tangible. Unveiled at TechCrunch Disrupt 2025, where it drew interest from companies including Sony. Now in production, launching October 2026, with open verification tools to follow (hoa.camera).

An AI painting assistant that preserves the artist’s identity
Lutra, 2025. Tech licensing to Urban Complex. Identity

Artists who use AI face the question from both sides: they wonder “is this still my work?”, and others deny the authorship outright. Building on our image AI, we repositioned the model from a creative tool that produces to an assistant grounded in the artist’s own identity, where the artist defines the boundaries of style and permitted derivation.

The technology was successfully licensed, and Urban Complex uses it in production today. Conducted as a joint TIPS R&D project between a financial-engineering lab at Yonsei University and Urban Complex; as Urban Complex's technology partner, I served as PI and led the research.

Image AI, between personalization and the uncanny
POSTECH, 2024. Capstone Design Project, 1st Place. Identity

A Stable Diffusion (LoRA + ControlNet) booth that dresses an existing character in your look. The character’s own identity has to survive, so the real question was how far the resemblance should go: which features, from hairstyle down, make people recognize themselves without the result feeling wrong.

With an HCI PhD researcher at KAIST, we ran about four months of consumer studies and surveys to find that point. Won 1st place in the POSTECH EE capstone design course, then commercialized as Character Studio.

A SaaS web builder with accessibility built in
Lutra, 2024. PageChips. Accessibility

Pages made with web builders routinely ignore web standards and accessibility. PageChips (chips.page) is a block-based SaaS builder that turns this around: every block ships with accessibility baked in, rules I learned first-hand while teaching at the Seoul National School for the Blind, so a page built fast is accessible by construction.

It never reached mass adoption, but at the Goyang International Flower Fair 2024 it served visitors from their 20s to their 70s: over 40,000 people used its maps and guides.

The safest, most joyful use of NFTs
Lutra, 2022. Clam. CES Innovation Award 2023. Play

When NFTs were new, opaque, and hyped, with interest running high even among older generations, we chose to lead the technology’s popularization in a good direction: a joyful, safe form. The concept was simple, turning people’s experiences into digital keepsakes they truly own.

Honored with a CES Innovation Award 2023 (Software & Mobile Apps) as the youngest Korean honoree. After CES, we signed MOUs with the blockchain subsidiaries of KAKAO and NAVER, and the product grew into a photo-booth form, supplied to institutions and brands. Still running at clamspace.com.

Predicting EV range: an accuracy problem or a UX problem?
Hyundai Motor Company, 2021. Big Data Team Internship. Trust

Range anxiety was a serious complaint: the dash says you can reach Busan, and the car stops near Daegu. I joined the big-data team to fix the Kona EV's range prediction with ML, and learned that perfect prediction is impossible, closer to forecasting stocks than measuring fuel. Studying how other makers’ interfaces handle this, I found they present short-horizon information built from the last minutes of driving, detailed enough that drivers see “it’s how I drive” rather than “the number is broken.”

My analysis identified which factors influence range the most, and the conclusion was that the problem must be solved jointly with UX: give drivers verifiable, short-horizon information instead of a single unexplained number.

MBTI × Face: a machine-learning test
POSTECH, 2021. Undergraduate Research Program, Excellence Award. Identity

“Physiognomy is just statistics,” people say. I tested the claim: MBTI-prediction models trained on celebrities’ photos and their publicly known types, reaching 76% accuracy on some traits.

Then I dug into why. The signal was confidence in the photo, its mood, the backgrounds, how heavy the makeup: where you spend your time and how you present yourself correlates with personality and leaks into photos. Not the face itself, a data-driven caution against physiognomic AI.

My role: leading the team, framing the research topic and direction, automating the face-detection and data pipeline, and exploring ML methods to set the approach.

Developing an NLP engine for public-opinion analysis
POSTECH, 2020. 3rd CCRP Competition Winner. Accessibility

Recommendation algorithms keep splitting public opinion and herding it to one side. I wanted the opposite: a way to see how each generation actually thinks about an issue. In the pre-LLM era, I built this with NLP, classifying sentiment and stance per issue, generation by generation.

The analysis engine itself was built and working, though never deployed in the field. Proposed as civic technology and selected and awarded in POSTECH’s CCRP (Convergence Civilization Renaissance Production) program.

Developing an educational humanoid robot for kids
ROBOTIS, 2020. Prototyping Team Internship. Play

A commercial line of educational humanoid robots, used in competitions like the STEAM Cup where kids code AI into their machines. My work covered the whole prototype: 3D printing, mechanical design, internal algorithms, and the core calibration question, how much power and range of motion the Dynamixel actuators should allow for a robot that looks cool to kids, invites customization, and battles safely.

I worked on the CEO’s direct new-product team, alongside engineers building autonomous robots, which gave me a wide view of prototyping across the company.

Developing a remote robot for the COVID era
POSTECH × DGIST, 2020. 8th K-Hackathon Award. Accessibility

When visiting in person became impossible, we built a robot to visit for you: touring a home before renting it, walking a museum from your couch. TurtleBot-based, with LiDAR and 160° cameras viewable in VR, controlled from an app. Pick a destination and it navigates there on its own, avoiding obstacles.

Awarded at the 8th K-Hackathon immersive-content challenge, and selected for the final live demonstration of the Soljik Challenge.

My role: leading the team, from setting the direction to the robot design and all of the ROS development.

A voting-information website for first-time voters in their 20s
Independent, 2020. Featured on KBS & MBC radio. Accessibility

As a first-time voter I kept asking why my generation ignores elections. My hypothesis: information that once reached everyone on paper is now scattered across the internet, complex and hard to read. With high-school friends, I built a single website that shows each candidate’s essentials as summary cards.

It reached 40,000 visitors, with thousands arriving daily at its peak, and was covered by newspapers and 20+ outlets, including radio interviews on KBS and MBC (govote.netlify.app).

Can blockchain solve low donation participation?
POSTECH, 2019. 2nd CCRP Competition Winner. Trust

I believed low giving comes from low trust in institutions. Operating costs are a fair use of donations; what’s missing is transparency about where the money goes. So I designed a public blockchain ledger that makes a charity’s inflows and outflows verifiable by anyone.

Built a simple working prototype, and the proposal was selected and awarded in POSTECH’s CCRP program.
